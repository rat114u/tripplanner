Oahu — The Gathering Place

2 nights · Jul 26–28 · Stay in Waikiki

Day 1 · Sun Jul 26
Arrive & Ease In
2–3 PM HST
Land at HNL. Collect bags, pick up rental car. Drive to Waikiki (~35 min). Check in to hotel — Ilikai Hotel or Outrigger Waikiki are solid mid-range picks.
4–6 PM
First look at the Pacific — stroll Waikiki Beach. Let your daughter dip her feet in. Simple, low-key, perfect after 11 hours of travel. kid-friendly
7 PM
Relaxed dinner near the hotel. Try Barefoot Beach Café or plate lunch from a local spot. Early bedtime — 6-hour time difference hits hard.
💡 Keep Day 1 very light. Jet lag from EST → HST is significant. A well-rested family on Day 2 is worth more than any extra sightseeing tonight.
Day 2 · Mon Jul 27
Diamond Head + Pearl Harbor
7:30 AM
Quick breakfast, head to Diamond Head Crater. Arrive by 8:30 AM before the heat peaks. 1.6-mile hike, ~1.5 hrs, paved but steep near the top. Views are spectacular. kid-friendly Book ahead at recreation.gov
10:30 AM
Post-hike snack / lunch break. Grab shave ice on the way back — Leonard's Bakery for malasadas if passing by!
12:30 PM
Pearl Harbor National Memorial. Audio tour of the USS Arizona Memorial is deeply moving — highly educational for a 10-year-old. Allow 2.5–3 hrs. Reserve free timed entry at recreation.gov
4 PM
Return to Waikiki. Hotel pool or beach to decompress. Let your daughter play in the water. kid-friendly
6:30 PM
Dinner at Tropics Bar & Grill (Hilton Hawaiian Village) — open-air, oceanfront, casual. Or explore Kalakaua Avenue food options.
💡 Pearl Harbor timed-entry tickets are free but mandatory — book the 1 PM slot now at recreation.gov. They sell out 2–3 months ahead in summer.

Big Island — Fire & Water

2 nights · Jul 28–30 · Stay near Volcano Village, then Kona

Day 3 · Tue Jul 28
Fly to Big Island + Volcanoes National Park
Morning
Early flight HNL → ITO (Hilo). Pick up rental car at Hilo airport. Grab lunch at Kamana Kitchen (1235 Kilauea Ave — authentic, excellent).
1 PM
Hawaii Volcanoes National Park. Start at Kilauea Visitor Center — great intro exhibits and maps. Then walk to Kilauea Iki Overlook for a jaw-dropping crater view. kid-friendly
2:30 PM
Thurston Lava Tube — a short walk through a dramatic ancient lava cave. Completely safe, well-lit, and kids absolutely love it. One of the best 20 minutes of the trip. kid-friendly
4 PM
Drive Chain of Craters Road — 20-mile scenic road through ancient lava flows to the ocean. Surreal landscape, stunning at golden hour. Stop at steam vents.
7 PM
Dinner at The Rim restaurant at Volcano House — perched on the caldera rim, views of Halema'uma'u crater are extraordinary. Reserve ahead
💡 Stay at Volcano House or a Volcano Village B&B — you're inside the national park, making early morning lava glow views easy to catch before the crowds.
Day 4 · Wed Jul 29
Waterfalls + Waipio + Drive to Kona
8 AM
Rainbow Falls in Hilo — 5-minute walk to a 80-foot waterfall. Best visited in the morning when rainbows form in the mist. Your daughter will love it. kid-friendly
9:30 AM
Akaka Falls State Park — a gorgeous 0.4-mile loop trail through bamboo and rainforest to two dramatic waterfalls (Akaka at 442 ft). One of Hawaii's finest. kid-friendly
11:30 AM
Grab lunch on the way. Begin the scenic Hamakua Coast drive toward Waimea (~1 hr north).
1:30 PM
Waipio Valley Lookout — pull over for a breathtaking panorama of a sacred valley, black sand beach, and waterfalls — no hike needed. kid-friendly
3 PM
Drive to Kona (~2 hrs via Highway 19). Check in at Aston Kona by the Sea — solid mid-range, ocean view lanais.
7 PM
Dinner at Swami's Dosa Grill in Kona — authentic South Indian vegetarian cuisine. Great option for the family.
🌺

Maui — The Valley Isle

2 nights · Jul 30 – Aug 1 · Stay near Lahaina / Kaanapali

Day 5 · Thu Jul 30
Fly to Maui + North Shore + Kaanapali
Morning
Morning flight KOA → OGG (possibly via HNL). Pick up rental car at Kahului airport. Check in to hotel near Lahaina.
1 PM
Kapalua Bay or Napili Bay — calm, crystal-clear, shallow water. Some of the best family-friendly snorkeling on Maui. Bring rented snorkel gear — sea turtles are common here! kid-friendly
3:30 PM
Whaler's Village on Kaanapali Beach — open-air shopping, shave ice, and a lovely ocean boardwalk. Casual and very kid-friendly. kid-friendly
6 PM
Sunset on Kaanapali Beach — one of the finest sunsets in Hawaii. The sky turns deep orange over the ocean and the island of Molokai.
7 PM
Dinner at Leilani's on the Beach — oceanfront, open-air, excellent fresh fish and kid-friendly burgers. Casual and beautiful.
Day 6 · Fri Jul 31
Haleakala Sunrise + Iao Valley + Evening Cruise
2:30 AM
Haleakala Sunrise — drive to the 10,023-ft summit (2 hrs from Lahaina). Watch the sun rise above the clouds over a Martian volcanic landscape. One of the most unforgettable experiences in all of Hawaii. Bring warm layers — it's 40°F at the top. Sunrise permits sell out weeks ahead — recreation.gov
7–10 AM
Return to hotel. Sleep / rest — this is intentional recovery time before the afternoon. The early wake-up is worth it, but rest is essential.
11 AM
Iao Valley State Monument — lush, dramatic valley with the 1,200-ft Iao Needle. Easy 0.6-mile paved trail. Kids love the green peaks rising from the mist. kid-friendly
1:30 PM
Maui Tropical Plantation — fun tram tour through a working plantation in Wailuku. Pineapples, coffee, macadamia nuts. Very family-friendly and educational. kid-friendly
5 PM
Optional: Sunset Snorkel Cruise from Lahaina Harbor — family-friendly 2-hr sailing trip. Several operators (Trilogy, Pride of Maui). Great final evening activity. Book ahead
8 PM
Final Hawaiian dinner in Lahaina. Pack bags tonight for early morning departure.
💡 Haleakala sunrise permits at recreation.gov typically sell out 2–3 months in advance. Book this first, before anything else on this list.
Day 7 · Sat Aug 1
Fly Home
Morning
Light breakfast, return rental car at OGG airport. Fly OGG → RDU (overnight or early morning with connection). Arrive home Aug 1–2 EST. Mahalo!

💰 Money-Saving Tips

1
Book Southwest for interisland — they frequently have $59 fares and include 2 free checked bags per person, saving ~$200 in baggage fees on the mainland leg too.
2
Book rental cars through Costco Travel or AutoSlash — can save 15–25% vs. booking direct, especially in peak season.
3
Use a credit card with travel insurance (Chase Sapphire, Amex Platinum) to skip the rental CDW — that's ~$15–20/day saved per car.
4
Avoid one-way car rentals on the Big Island (Hilo pickup / Kona drop) if possible — the one-way fee can add $50–100. Consider dropping the car in Hilo and taking a shuttle to your Kona hotel.
5
July is the single most expensive month for Hawaii travel — booking now (April 2026) vs. waiting 6–8 weeks could save $200–400 on the mainland flight alone.
